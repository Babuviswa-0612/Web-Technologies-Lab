Parts of Camera knowmore

A camera functions by controlling light through a lens, directing it onto a sensor or film to capture images. Essential parts include the lens, aperture, shutter, and image sensor. Cameras have evolved significantly, transitioning from film-based systems to digital technologies, which offer superior image quality, storage, and ease of use. Modern cameras boast features like autofocus, image stabilization, and digital processing, making photography more accessible and versatile. This technological advancement has revolutionized both professional and consumer photography, enhancing creativity and convenience.

Greatest Directors of All Time

  1. Hollywood

    • Alfred Hitchcock: Known for thrillers like Psycho, Rear Window, and Vertigo.
    • Stanley Kubrick: Directed films such as 2001: A Space Odyssey, A Clockwork Orange, and The Shining.
    • Martin Scorsese: Famous for movies like Taxi Driver, Goodfellas, and The Wolf of Wall Street.
    • Akira Kurosawa: Known for classics such as Seven Samurai, Rashomon, and Ran.
    • Steven Spielberg: Directed iconic films like Jaws, E.T., and Schindler's List.
    • Ingmar Bergman: Known for existential works like The Seventh Seal and Wild Strawberries.
    • Quentin Tarantino: Celebrated for films like Pulp Fiction, Kill Bill, and Inglourious Basterds.
    • Orson Welles: Revolutionized cinema with Citizen Kane.
    • Francis Ford Coppola: Best known for The Godfather trilogy and Apocalypse Now.
    • Federico Fellini: Renowned for films like La Dolce Vita and 8½.

  2. Bollywood

    • Raj Kapoor: Directed classics like Awaara, Shree 420, and Sangam.
    • Yash Chopra: Known for romantic films like Kabhi Kabhie, Silsila, and Veer-Zaara.
    • Guru Dutt: Directed cult classics like Pyaasa, Kaagaz Ke Phool, and Sahib Bibi Aur Ghulam.
    • Ramesh Sippy: Best known for the iconic film Sholay and other hits like Seeta Aur Geeta.
    • Mani Ratnam: Directed films such as Roja, Bombay, and Guru, blending commercial success with deep storytelling.
    • Rajkumar Hirani: Famous for films like 3 Idiots, PK, and Munna Bhai MBBS, mixing humor with social messages.
    • Vishal Bhardwaj: Known for his adaptations of Shakespeare, including Maqbool, Omkara, and Haider.
    • Karan Johar: Known for modern classics like Kuch Kuch Hota Hai, Kabhi Khushi Kabhie Gham, and My Name is Khan.
    • Anurag Kashyap: Director of edgy films like Gangs of Wasseypur, Black Friday, and Dev D.
    • Mehboob Khan: Directed the epic film Mother India, one of Bollywood's most iconic films.

  3. Kollywood (Tamil Cinema)

    • Mani Ratnam: Known for films like Nayakan, Roja, and Alaipayuthey, blending deep storytelling with strong visuals.
    • Shankar: Famous for blockbuster hits like Indian, Enthiran, and Anniyan, combining visual grandeur with social messages.
    • Bala: Known for intense, critically acclaimed films like Sethu, Pithamagan, and Naan Kadavul.
    • K. Balachander: A legendary filmmaker, known for films like Apoorva Raagangal and Avargal, and for introducing talents like Rajinikanth and Kamal Haasan.
    • Gautham Vasudev Menon: Celebrated for romantic and thriller films like Vinnaithaandi Varuvaayaa, Kaakha Kaakha, and Vettaiyaadu Vilaiyaadu.
    • Vetrimaaran: Known for realistic films like Visaranai, Aadukalam, and Asuran, blending raw emotion with powerful narratives.
    • Karthik Subbaraj: Known for fresh storytelling in films like Pizza, Jigarthanda, and Petta.
